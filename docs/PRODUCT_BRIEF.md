# KithFun — Product Brief

_A social metaverse for university campuses. Campus Quest is the backbone; social connection is the hook._

## 1. The Core Loop (The Backbone)

- **The Map** — a high-fidelity, interactive map of the campus.
- **The Factions** — every user is sorted into a "House" (by dorm, faculty, or year). Actions (quests, event attendance) earn points for your House.
- **Fog of War** — parts of the campus map start hidden. You unlock regions by physically visiting them, making exploration the primary hook.
- **Daily rhythm** — daily quests, streak rewards, and faction leaderboards form the 3-step retention loop.

## 2. The Social & Dating Layer (The Spark)

Connections are contextual and competitive, not swipe-and-ghost:

- **Quest-Based Matching** — when two people match, the app issues a **Dual Quest** instead of opening a chat. Example: _"To unlock your chat, both of you must reach [the library courtyard] within the next 2 hours."_ It kills the awkward "hey" and turns the first meeting into a collaborative game.
- **Quest Compatibility** — the map highlights "Quest Allies": people currently working on the same kind of quests or sharing interests (design, trading, community projects).
- **Meetup Spots** — the app suggests Quest Zones for first meetings: safe, public, interesting spots on campus.

## 3. Sub-Arenas (Pluggable Competitive Hubs)

Physical locations on the map host virtual arenas:

### Trading Battleground (Fantasy Finance)
- Virtual-balance trading leagues: compete on ROI over 24-hour windows (Forex, crypto, NSE stocks).
- Leaderboards, chat rooms, badges ("Risk Taker", "Consistent Gainer", "Market Maverick").
- No real money at risk — pure competitive sport.

### Design Arena
- Users post sketches, 3D models, furniture/fashion prototypes.
- Tinder-style **swipe-to-vote** on aesthetics, sustainability, technical feasibility.
- Winners get homepage features and campus currency to unlock premium templates.

### Predict & Prove (Social Prop-Bets)
- Private rooms where friends post predictions on anything — EPL matches, student elections, which lecture gets cancelled.
- No money: the stake is **social capital**. An all-time "Best Oracle" leaderboard captures the "I told you so" glory.

## 4. Social Capital Economy

- **Campus Currency** earned by completing quests and high-value interactions (collaborative design, academic assistance, community development work).
- Spend it on: profile customization (virtual furniture/avatar items), access to exclusive Networking Zones, premium design templates.
- Tradeable between students for services (tutoring, typing assistance) — keeps value circulating inside the student ecosystem.

## 5. Engagement Mechanics (The Secret Sauce)

- **Streaks** — daily participation streaks; humans hate breaking them.
- **Social validation** — every action (trade, design post, quest win) is one-tap shareable to WhatsApp/social media.
- **Asymmetry** — easy to consume (vote), challenging to produce (create). Creators become campus celebrities.
- **Faction pressure** — your House needs your points; your friends recruit you.

## 6. Positioning & Vibe

- **Aesthetic** — sleek, dark-themed UI (trading-terminal energy) with vibrant, playful avatars.
- **Onboarding** — feels like a secret-society induction. You're not joining a dating app; you're joining the campus elite.
- **Trust-first** — verified university ID at signup. Real students only.

## 7. Market Notes

- **Target** — university students in Kenya; mobile-first.
- **Local moat** — M-Pesa integration for any real-money features, student/national ID verification, low-latency design for local network conditions.
- **Monetization (ethical)** — native to the game: cosmetic campus-currency purchases, exclusive zones, sponsored quests from campus businesses. Never pay-to-win, never real-money gambling.

## 8. MVP Direction (to be scoped)

Priority order (draft):
1. Campus map + quest engine (location check-ins, fog of war)
2. Faction system + leaderboards + streaks
3. Dual Quest matching flow
4. One sub-arena (pick the one with the strongest early community — likely Predict & Prove, cheapest to build)
5. Campus currency (ledger only, no purchases yet)

Open decisions: tech stack (Flutter vs React Native for mobile; Python/FastAPI vs Node for backend), map provider, geofencing accuracy on campus, ID verification flow.
